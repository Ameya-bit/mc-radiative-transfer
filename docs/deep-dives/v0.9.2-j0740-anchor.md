## 1. What this anchor asks

v0.9.0 measured the beaming systematic `ΔPF = PF_real − PF_iso` on invented always-visible
geometries (up to +0.16). v0.9.1 planted the same swap at J0030 and found it saturated
to ≈ 0 — J0030's spots eclipse, pinning PF at 1, and the systematic moved into the
waveform shape. That left the obvious question: **does a real, published geometry ever
let the systematic land directly in the pulsed fraction NICER actually reports?**

PSR J0740+6620 — the high-mass (≈ 2 M⊙) NICER mass–radius pulsar — is the natural place
to look. We take two independent fits and run the identical isotropic→realistic swap:

- **Riley et al. (2021, ApJL 918 L27)** — X-PSI / Amsterdam, ST-U model (primary).
- **Miller et al. (2021, ApJL 918 L28)** — Illinois–Maryland, two-circle (cross-check).

As in v0.9.1 this is **differential, not a fit**: we adopt each fit's spacetime
(`u = 2GM/Rc²`), inclination, spot colatitudes, sizes, temperatures, and phase
separation, and change only the beaming term.

| | M (M⊙) | R (km) | u | i | spot colatitudes | Δφ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Riley 2021 (ST-U, headline) | 2.072 | 12.39 | 0.494 | 87.6° | 77.3°, 108.3° | 0.442 |
| Miller 2021 (2-circle, Table 7) | 2.062 | 13.71 | 0.444 | 87.5° | 91.7°, 92.4° | 0.558 |

---

## 2. Why J0740 is the complement of J0030

Two features make J0740 the opposite case from J0030.

**The spots never set (Riley).** J0740 is viewed almost edge-on (i ≈ 87.6°), but it is
*extremely* compact (u ≈ 0.494). Beloborodov bending at that compactness lets the observer
see ψ up to ≈ 167° around the back — far enough that Riley's spots, at colatitudes 77° and
108°, only ever **graze** the limb (μ_min ≈ 0.005) instead of diving behind it. The
single-spot eclipse fraction is **0%** for both spots (`single_spot_eclipsed_fraction`
returns 0.0); contrast J0030's 45%.

**The spots tile the rotation.** Riley's two spots sit in *opposite* hemispheres and are
~half a cycle apart in azimuth (Δφ ≈ 0.442). So when one spot grazes the limb the other
faces us: the summed flux never falls near zero. The floor is `F_min/F_max ≈ 0.70` — a
double-peaked pulse, not an eclipse. This is exactly what J0030 could *not* do: there both
spots hug the same far hemisphere, so even ~0.45 cycle apart their dark windows overlap
and the flux still touches zero.

A pulse with a high, non-zero floor has a **low, unsaturated** pulsed fraction —
`PF_iso ≈ 0.178`. That is the headroom J0030 lacked.

---

## 3. The result: ΔPF ≈ +0.16, directly in the pulsed fraction

With PF unsaturated, the isotropic→realistic swap shows up where NICER reads it. Limb
darkening multiplies the bright phase (a spot facing us, μ → 1) more than the faint phase
(a spot at the limb, μ → 0), so the pulse **sharpens** and PF rises:

| τ | Riley ΔPF | Miller ΔPF |
|---|---|---|
| 0.1 | +0.025 | +0.036 |
| 1 | +0.104 | +0.160 |
| 10 | **+0.164** | **+0.229** |
| 30 | +0.153 | +0.218 |

The Riley headline is **ΔPF = +0.164 at τ ≈ 10** (PF rises from 0.178 to 0.342) — the
same size, **same positive sign**, and same τ-dependence (peaking near τ ≈ 10 where the
limb-darkening slope `b(τ)` peaks, easing by τ = 30) as v0.9.0's invented geometries. The
difference is that this is a **published** geometry, and the effect is **PF-visible**.

![Left: ΔPF rises with τ to +0.16 (Riley) / +0.23 (Miller) — the beaming systematic in the pulsed fraction. Center: the non-eclipsing double-peaked Riley pulse (floor 0.70) sharpened by realistic beaming. Right: an assumed background only dilutes the positive ΔPF.](../../data/pulse_profile_j0740.png)

## 4. Both teams agree — and what that teaches about the discriminator

Miller's independent fit agrees with Riley on M, R, inclination, anti-phasing, and the
two-circular-spot model, but places the spots essentially **on the equator** (colatitude
≈ 92°) — the parameter both teams flag as least constrained. With an equatorial observer
each spot's *center* is hidden for **~21%** of the cycle: by the single-spot criterion,
Miller's J0740 spots eclipse.

And yet **PF does not saturate**. Because the two spots are anti-phased, their eclipses
fall at opposite phases; the combined pulse floor is still `F_min/F_max ≈ 0.63`, PF_iso ≈
0.227, and the swap lands an even larger **ΔPF ≈ +0.229**. So J0740's headline is robust
to which team's geometry you adopt: the systematic is a +0.16…+0.23 pulsed-fraction effect
either way.

This sharpens the lesson from v0.9.1. The thing that flips J0030's verdict is **not**
whether a single spot eclipses — Miller's J0740 spots eclipse and still give a live ΔPF.
It is whether the spots **tile** the rotation:

- **J0030** — two spots in the *same* far hemisphere, each hidden ~45%: their dark windows
  overlap, the flux hits zero, PF pins at 1, ΔPF ≈ 0 → systematic in waveform shape.
- **J0740** — two spots anti-phased in *opposite* regions, shallow enough that one always
  faces us: the flux stays off zero, PF keeps its headroom, ΔPF ≈ +0.16…+0.23 →
  systematic in the pulsed fraction.

`test_j0740_miller_spots_eclipse_yet_combined_pulse_does_not_saturate` locks this in: a
single Miller spot's flux reaches exactly zero, but the anti-phased two-spot sum does not.

---

## 5. Robustness: a background only dilutes the signal

J0030 needed a background *caveat* — its ΔPF was zero until an assumed background
un-saturated the pinned PF, and the re-exposed systematic was sign-flipped. J0740 needs no
such rescue: the systematic is already live at B = 0. Adding a common unpulsed background
only **dilutes** it, and it stays positive:

| background (fraction of peak) | Riley ΔPF | Miller ΔPF |
|---|---|---|
| 0% | +0.164 | +0.229 |
| 5% | +0.149 | +0.209 |
| 30% | +0.102 | +0.145 |

So unlike J0030's, J0740's PF systematic is not a knife-edge artifact of a hard-zero floor;
it survives a realistic background with the same sign.

## 6. Honest scope (carried from v0.9.1)

- **Point spots, not the published caps.** We reduce each fit's spots to points at their
  center colatitudes. J0740's ST-U / two-circle spots are small circular caps (ζ ≈ 0.1–0.15
  rad), so the point reduction is cleaner here than for J0030's crescent — `sin ζ ≈ ζ` to
  < 0.5% — but it still over-sharpens grazing edges slightly.
- **Weighting.** Bolometric weight ∝ area(sin²ζ) × T⁴ from each table; the two caps are
  near-identical in size and temperature, so the result barely depends on it.
- **Phase is bimodal (Riley).** The ML-point separation Δφ ≈ 0.442 is used; the posterior
  is flagged multi-modal, but the anti-phased mode is the physical one and what we adopt.
- **Differential, not a fit; monochromatic / bolometric, conservative Thomson,
  slow-rotation Schwarzschild** — the same idealizations as v0.9.0/v0.9.1.

## 7. Status

- **Result delivered:** at J0740's published geometry the point-spot pulse is
  non-eclipsing and unsaturated (Riley PF_iso = 0.178, floor 0.70), so the beaming
  systematic lands directly in the pulsed fraction: **ΔPF = +0.164 at τ ≈ 10** (Riley),
  **+0.229** (Miller) — same sign and τ-shape as v0.9.0, now on a real star and confirmed
  by both teams.
- **The narrative completes the arc:** the systematic's observability is geometry-
  dependent, and the knob is *tiling*. PF-visible at J0740 (anti-phased, opposite regions),
  shape-only at J0030 (same hemisphere, deep eclipses).
- **Tests: 47/47 pass** (four new: single spot never eclipses, anti-phased spots tile and
  stay off zero, beaming raises PF, and Miller's spots eclipse yet the sum does not
  saturate).
- **No core change** — two-spot synthesis is script-level, now via the shared
  `scripts/anchor_lib.py` (J0030 reproduces its v0.9.1 numbers bit-for-bit after the
  extraction).

**Next:** v1.0.0 — the paper. The three rungs now tell one story: the beaming systematic
is real and ≈ +0.16 in PF (v0.9.0), it hides in waveform shape when the geometry eclipses
(v0.9.1, J0030), and it lands back in the pulsed fraction when the spots tile the rotation
(v0.9.2, J0740).
