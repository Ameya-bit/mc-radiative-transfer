## 1. Why validate in the antipodal limit

The phase diagram's novelty is the equal-colatitude, swept-separation slice — which is
*not* what the standard pulse-profile literature maps. The way to trust a novel extension
is to show the machinery reproduces the canonical result in the limit where the canonical
result is established. For two hot spots that limit is the **antipodal** configuration
(colatitudes θ and 180−θ, half a cycle apart), and the canonical result is the Poutanen &
Beloborodov (2006) visibility classification.

## 2. Tier 1 — the single-spot eclipse onset (Beloborodov)

A point spot's emission cosine is `cos α = u + (1−u) cos ψ`; it is visible while cos α ≥ 0,
i.e. `cos ψ ≥ −u/(1−u)`. Over a rotation cos ψ is smallest at the farthest phase (φ = π),
where cos ψ = cos(i + θ). So the spot eclipses for some phase **exactly when**

    cos(i + θ)  <  −u / (1 − u)      [ = mcrt.visibility_threshold(u) ].

`beloborodov_grid_mismatches` sweeps a grid of (i, θ, u) and confirms the *engine's* eclipse
(does the simulated visibility mask ever go dark?) flips precisely at this analytic
condition: **0 mismatches** across the whole grid. This is the v0.8.0 Rung-A check, now
verified everywhere rather than at a few points.

## 3. Tier 2 — the antipodal visibility classes (PB06 Fig. 5)

For an antipodal pair the secondary mirrors the primary: `cos ψ₂(φ) = −cos ψ₁(φ)`. With the
symmetric bending threshold c ≡ u/(1−u), the primary is visible where cos ψ₁ ≥ −c and the
antipode where cos ψ₁ ≤ c. Since the primary's cos ψ spans [m, M] = [cos(i+θ), cos(i−θ)],
how that interval sits relative to the both-visible band [−c, c] gives four classes —
exactly Poutanen & Beloborodov (2006)'s Fig. 5 (verified against the paper):

| Class | Meaning | Closed form |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | a single spot visible all rotation; the antipode never rises | [m, M] ∩ [−c, c] = ∅ |
| **II** | one spot always visible; the antipode eclipses for part of the cycle | one endpoint inside [−c, c] |
| **III** | each spot eclipses for part of the cycle, never both gone at once | [−c, c] ⊂ (m, M) |
| **IV** | both spots visible all rotation (a flat plateau for blackbody spots) | [m, M] ⊆ [−c, c] |

(An antipodal pair always shows at least one spot — there is no total eclipse — so the
visible-spot count never drops to zero, the n_min = 1 floor PB06 note.) The boundary m = −c
is the *same* Beloborodov condition as Tier 1.

`class_maps` classifies every (i, θ) cell two independent ways — analytically from (m, M, c),
and numerically by counting the engine's visible-spot mask per phase — and confirms they
agree on **all** cells, with all four classes present. The figure's two left panels are the
analytic and engine class maps; they are visually identical. Reproducing the canonical map
in the antipodal limit is what licenses the non-antipodal, swept-Δφ extension of v0.9.4.

## 4. Positioning against Zhao, Psaltis & Özel (2024)

Zhao et al. (2024; arXiv:2412.12283, 2412.12284) are the closest prior work: they study the
*same* wrong-beaming systematic on a two-spot star. The distinction (verified against both
papers' full text) is what they measure and vary:

- **Observable.** They expand the waveform in Fourier harmonics and track the fractional
  amplitude **A₁/A₀**, routing the beaming systematic into inferred **compactness / radius**.
  The term "pulsed fraction" does not appear in either paper.
- **Geometry.** They fix **two antipodal spots** (second spot's longitude locked at +180°)
  and vary only inclination and colatitude — they do **not** sweep the azimuthal separation.
- **Criterion.** No tiling / PF-saturation argument appears.

The figure's third panel is the direct side-by-side: on Zhao's own antipodal geometry, we
add the three layers they omit — the **pulsed fraction**, a **swept azimuthal separation**
Δφ, and the **tiling** reading. PF_iso falls from ~0.76 (spots overlapping, Δφ = 0) toward
~0 (spots tiling the rotation, Δφ = 0.5), while the realistic beaming holds PF_real higher,
so ΔPF stays positive and *varies with separation* — the axis Zhao's harmonic analysis never
uses. (Bauböck et al. 2015, the single-spot spot-*size* foil, likewise contains no two-spot
tiling argument.)

## 5. A note carried to the paper

Verifying PB06 surfaced one prose precision point: the slow-rotation bolometric flux is
`F ∝ (1−u) · I(cos α) · cos α · d(cos α)/d(cos ψ)`, and the bending Jacobian
`d(cos α)/d(cos ψ) = (1−u)` is itself constant — so the net compactness prefactor is `(1−u)²`,
not a single `(1−u)`. Because it is a phase-independent constant it **cancels in every
ratio-based observable** (pulsed fraction, waveform shape, ΔPF), so no result here or in
v0.9.0–v0.9.4 changes. The engine's `point_spot_flux` carries the explicit `(1−u)` and is
documented as proportional ("see PB06 for the full expression"); the v1.0.0 paper should
write the Jacobian explicitly so the normalization is unambiguous.
