## 1. Why the point reduction is most suspect exactly where it matters

The pulsed-fraction result is exact for a point spot only if the emission cosine μ = cos α is
uniform across the real, finite cap. It is least accurate where μ varies fastest across the
spot — at **grazing angles** (μ → 0, the limb). That is precisely the J0740 regime: its spots
skim the limb (μ_min ≈ 0.005), and limb darkening I(μ) has its steepest leverage there. So the
headline +0.16 is the result where "it's just a point" most deserves a check. Rather than
assert it, we measure it.

## 2. Tiling a cap

A circular spot of angular radius ζ centered at (θ_c, φ_c) is covered by concentric rings of
sub-points. Each ring at offset ϑ from the cap center carries its true spherical surface area
(∝ cos of the ring's inner/outer edges), so the weighting is area-correct; the sub-points are
placed by rotating the offset around the cap-center direction in an orthonormal tangent basis,
then read back as (colatitude, longitude). Two properties make this a clean check:

- **ζ → 0 reproduces the point spot** exactly (the tiler returns the single center point), so
  the finite-cap curve is continuous with the existing anchor result.
- **Each sub-point is one `compute_profile` call**, rolled by its own longitude and summed with
  its area weight — the same additive, no-core-change machinery as the anchors.

Refining the tiling (5 rings × 16 azimuths → 10 × 32) must not move ΔPF; the script reports that
refinement delta so the number is a converged bound, not a sampling estimate.

## 3. The measured bound

At each anchor's published cap radius, τ = 10:

| Anchor | ζ̄ (rad) | ΔPF point | ΔPF finite cap | shift | refine Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| J0740 Riley 2021 | 0.146 | +0.164 | +0.161 | −0.003 | ~0 |
| J0740 Miller 2021 | 0.097 | +0.229 | +0.226 | −0.003 | ~0 |
| J0030 Riley 2019 | 0.150* | +0.000 | +0.000 | 0.000 | ~0 |
| J0030 Miller 2019 | 0.035 | +0.000 | +0.000 | 0.000 | ~0 |

\* J0030 Riley's ST+PST radii are not cleanly tabulated (the PST is a crescent), so a
conservative ζ = 0.15 rad upper bound is used — as large as J0740's caps. The true spots are
smaller, so this *over*-states the finite-size effect on the (already saturated) J0030 verdict.

The finite-size bias is **−0.003** at the published J0740 spot sizes — about 2% of the +0.16
signal — and the refinement delta is ≈ 0, so the tiling is converged. The ΔPF-vs-ζ curve (figure,
left panel) shows the bias grows slowly and monotonically with spot size: even at an
exaggerated ζ = 0.2 rad the J0740 result only slips to +0.159 (Riley) / +0.216 (Miller). J0030
stays pinned at ΔPF ≈ 0 even at the ζ = 0.15 upper bound — finite spots do not un-saturate it.

## 4. What this licenses

The sign, size, and τ-shape of the systematic hold under realistic spot size: **J0740 stays a
live, positive pulsed-fraction systematic (+0.16…+0.23); J0030 stays saturated (≈ 0).** The
point reduction is therefore an accurate approximation with a now-measured ≲ 0.003 bound, not an
unverified caveat. And the phase-diagram tiling/saturation *boundary* (v0.9.4) is even more
robust than the exact ΔPF: it is set by spot **visibility**, not by the cap interior, so it does
not move with ζ at all.
