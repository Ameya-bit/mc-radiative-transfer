## 1. Why Track C exists

The stars are fast, and our model froze them. Everything through v0.9.9 ran in the
**slow-rotation (Schwarzschild) limit**: the spot's *position* sweeps with phase but its
*velocity* is ignored. That is exact only as ν → 0. J0740's surface actually moves at
**β ≈ 0.12 c**; a referee will ask what omitting that cost the ΔPF systematic. Track C
answers it by *measuring* the difference, the same move Track B made for light bending.

The one term that can matter is subtle. Doppler splits into two pieces:
- the **boost** δⁿ, which brightens the approaching phase and dims the receding one, and
- the **aberration** μ′ = δ cos α, which shifts the *angle* at which the beaming law I(μ) is
  sampled.

The boost is the *same* function of phase for the isotropic and realistic models (it depends
on geometry + velocity, not on beaming), so the naïve expectation was that it cancels in the
*difference* ΔPF = PF_real − PF_iso and only aberration survives. **That expectation was
wrong** — see §6.

## 2. The β correction (a plan-doc error, now fixed in code)

`next-steps.md` quoted "β ≈ 0.088 Riley, ≈ 0.10 Miller." Those numbers **dropped the √(1−u)
gravitational-redshift enhancement** — they are the coordinate speed 2πνR sinθ/c, not the
locally measured speed the Doppler formula needs (Bogdanov eq. 11):

    β(θ) = 2πν R sinθ / (c √(1 − u))

The correct values are **β_eq ≈ 0.127 (Riley, u = 0.494)** and **≈ 0.133 (Miller, u = 0.444)** —
about 40 % larger than the plan's estimate, so the effect is *stronger*, not weaker. The code
uses the correct form (`mcrt.rotating.spot_speed`); the roadmap line should be corrected when
this lands.

## 3. C1 — the Doppler + aberration layer (`mcrt.rotating`)

Implements the Schwarzschild + Doppler (S+D) recipe of **Bogdanov et al. 2019 (ApJL 887,
L26)** — the same paper as the existing SD1a code-comparison. Equation numbers are theirs:

| quantity | expression | eq. |
|---|---|---|
| surface speed | β(θ) = 2πνR sinθ / (c√(1−u)),  γ = 1/√(1−β²) | 11 |
| ray/velocity angle | cos ξ = − sin α · sin i · sin φ / sin ψ | (derived) |
| Doppler factor | δ = 1 / [γ (1 − β cos ξ)] | 12 |
| aberration | cos α′ = δ cos α  → sample I at μ′ = δ cos α | 13 |
| observed flux | dF(E) = (1−u)^½ δ³ I′(E′,α′) cos α′ · (d cosα/d cosψ) · dS′/D² | 20 |

Integrating eq. 20 over observed energy (our library I(μ) is grey / energy-integrated) gives
the production flux the layer applies:

    F(φ) ∝ γ(θ) · δⁿ · I(μ′) · μ′ · D,   μ′ = δ cos α,   n = 4 (energy) / 3 (photon)

with D = d cosα/d cosψ the lensing Jacobian (from `pulse`/`bending`). The projection cosine is
the *comoving* μ′ (via the Lorentz invariant dS cosα = dS′ cosα′, eq. 14). `Rotation.photon_flux`
switches n = 4 → 3. **ν → 0 recovers the frozen flux bit-for-bit** (β → 0 ⇒ δ, γ → 1,
μ′ → cos α), verified by `np.array_equal`. Wired into `pulse.point_spot_flux` /
`compute_profile` and `anchor_lib.multi_spot_flux` via an optional `rotation=` arg (off by
default; `cos_alpha` stays the static geometric cosine, so the beaming/rotation swaps stay
controlled experiments).

**Deliberately excluded** (geometry-only, cancel differentially in ΔPF — kept as quantified
caveats, per the plan): photon light-travel-time delay (eq. 18–19, ~2 % of phase) and
rotational oblateness (~3 % at 346 Hz, AlGendy & Morsink 2014).

## 4. C1 validation — the rotating SD1c waveform

The decisive external check is **SD1c** (Bogdanov Table 1): a *point* spot at θ_c = ζ = 90°,
M = 1.4 M⊙, R = 12 km, **200 Hz**, blackbody kT = 0.35 keV, isotropic — the *same geometry as
the existing SD1a (1 Hz) test but rotating*, so it isolates pure Doppler + aberration. Driver
`scripts/c1_doppler_validate.py`; reference `data/l26_reference/SD1c_test_IM.txt`.

For a blackbody the monochromatic flux collapses cleanly (the δ³ boost cancels the E′³ Planck
head), leaving f(φ) ∝ cos α′ · D / (exp[E′/kT] − 1), E′ = 1 keV/(δ√(1−u)).

| | max\|Δ\| | RMS | fore-aft asymmetry (ours / ref) |
|---|---|---|---|
| SD1a (1 Hz, static) | 0.81 % | 0.17 % | 0.07 % / 0.07 % |
| **SD1c (200 Hz)** | **1.36 %** | **0.22 %** | **13.99 % / 14.03 %** |

The layer carries SD1a's ~1 % linear-bending agreement to 200 Hz and reproduces the 14 %
pulse skew almost exactly. **Note:** reproducing SD1c required applying the light-travel-time
delay (~0.008 cyc at 200 Hz; without it the residual is 7.6 %) — geometry-only, so it lives
*only in the validation script*, not the production layer. Independent machine-precision check:
a from-scratch 4-vector Lorentz boost reproduces δ and μ′ across geometries (`test_rotating.py`).

## 5. C2 — the measurement (`scripts/c2_doppler_coupling.py`)

Same per-seed *difference-of-differences* structure as B2, on the production 5-seed library
with exact bending, iso and real sharing the same Doppler treatment. δ⁴ energy boost:

| anchor (346.5 Hz) | β_eq | ΔPF(0 Hz) | ΔPF(346.5 Hz) | coupling | \|Δ\|/σ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Riley J0740** (u=0.494) | 0.127 | +0.1371 ± 0.0027 | **+0.0371 ± 0.0021** | −0.1000 | 37σ |
| **Miller J0740** (u=0.444) | 0.134 | +0.1952 ± 0.0053 | **+0.0612 ± 0.0032** | −0.1340 | 25σ |

**J0030 (eclipsing) is unmoved:** ΔPF ≈ 0 at both 0 and 205.5 Hz — rotation cannot un-eclipse
a spot (visibility cos α ≥ 0 is a static-frame condition), so PF stays pinned at 1 and the
systematic stays shape-only. Good sanity check.

**It is not a bug.** The coupling scales smoothly and monotonically with spin and vanishes
exactly at ν = 0:

| ν (Hz) | 0 | 50 | 100 | 200 | 346.5 | 600 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ΔPF (Riley, δ⁴) | +0.137 | +0.116 | +0.100 | +0.071 | +0.037 | +0.019 |

**Decomposition (Riley, 346.5 Hz)** — the boost, not aberration, is the driver:

| effect | ΔPF | coupling |
|---|---|---|
| aberration only (μ′ = δ cos α, no boost) | +0.115 | −0.022 |
| **δ⁴ boost only** | **+0.034** | **−0.103** |
| full | +0.037 | −0.100 |

Aberration is the modest term originally expected to matter. The **flux boost** dominates,
and it is leading-order: δ⁴ ≈ 1 + 4β cos ξ, so at β ≈ 0.12 the boost swings the flux by
**~±50 %**, dwarfing a pulse whose intrinsic PF is only 0.17.

**δ³ (photon) vs δ⁴ (energy):** δ³ is milder but still large — Riley 346.5 Hz gives +0.054
(δ³) vs +0.037 (δ⁴). A real ~0.02 ambiguity from the flux convention alone — **superseded by
the §5c band measurement**, which contains both as wide-band limits.

### 5b. Shape routing — the systematic re-routes, it does not vanish

The ΔPF collapse is a property of the **min/max statistic**, not of the beaming signal.
Companion measurement (now part of `c2_doppler_coupling.py`; pooled curve, exact bending, δ⁴;
four-pulse figure `data/pulse_profile_doppler_routing.png`):

| Riley (u=0.494) | PF_iso | PF_real | ΔPF | shape RMS | Δ(A1/A0) | Δ(A2/A0) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 Hz | 0.172 | 0.309 | +0.137 | **0.107** | +0.033 | +0.137 |
| 346.5 Hz | 0.386 | 0.423 | +0.037 | **0.105** | +0.029 | +0.066 |

(Miller: shape RMS 0.125 → 0.107, Δ(A1/A0) +0.027 → +0.024.) Three reads:
1. The **peak-normalized waveform difference between iso and real is spin-invariant** — a
   waveform-fitting analysis (which is what the NICER teams do; PF is only our summary
   statistic) sees an essentially undiminished cost of assuming isotropy.
2. The **fundamental harmonic keeps most of the signal**: Δ(A1/A0) drops ~12%, not ~73%.
   What spin actually does is pump a large *common* second harmonic into both models
   (A2/A0 iso: 0.02 → 0.19 — the once-per-cycle boost multiplying a once-per-cycle pulse),
   and the min/max PF feels that common term at both extremes, so the two PFs converge.
3. This is the **same routing logic as J0030**: geometry (eclipse/dark gap) routes the
   systematic from PF into shape; spin does it too, by a different mechanism (statistic
   saturation, not pinning). Two routers, one conserved waveform-level systematic.

### 5c. C3 — the band-limited number (`scripts/c3_band_doppler.py`)

`mcrt.rotating.BandSpectrum`/`band_boost`: for a comoving Planck spectrum every
Doppler/redshift factor collapses into the effective observed temperature
T_obs = δ√(1−u)·kT (the δ³ boost cancels the E′³ Planck head — the same collapse SD1c
uses), so in-band counts weight the pulse by Φ₂(δ)/Φ₂(1),
Φ_k(δ) = ∫_{E1}^{E2} E^k dE/(exp[E/(δ√(1−u)kT)]−1); wide-band limits recover δ³/δ⁴
exactly (tested). Inputs: kT = 0.0842 keV (Riley, log₁₀T = 5.99) / 0.094 keV (Miller);
calibrated NICER bands 0.3–1.5 keV (Riley, channels [30,150)) / 0.3–1.24 keV (Miller,
channels 30–123). The band sits on the **Wien tail** (E/kT_obs ≈ 5–25): in-band effective
exponent **n_eff = d lnΦ/d lnδ ≈ 6.1 (Riley) / 5.5 (Miller)** — steeper than δ⁴, so the
§8.2 direction argument is confirmed and the dilution **deepens**:

| ΔPF(τ=10, 346.5 Hz) | 0 Hz | δ³ photon | δ⁴ energy | band energy | **band photon (NICER)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Riley | +0.1371 ± 0.0027 | +0.0544 | +0.0371 | +0.0168 | **+0.0192 ± 0.0020** |
| Miller | +0.1952 ± 0.0053 | +0.0757 | +0.0612 | +0.0409 | **+0.0446 ± 0.0027** |

Robustness (Riley): band-edge scan +0.009 (e_min 0.4) … +0.024 (e_min 0.25) — e_max is
irrelevant (Wien tail: the counts live at the soft edge), e_min is the lever; kT scan
0.07 → 0.10 keV gives +0.013 → +0.024. So the residual PF systematic is both **small and
band/kT-sensitive** — it cannot carry a headline, which is itself evidence for the routing
framing. **In-band shape routing intact:** shape RMS 0.106, Δ(A1/A0) +0.024 (Riley) —
the conserved quantity survives the band treatment.

### 5d. C4 — caveat re-audit against the diluted number (`scripts/c4_caveat_audit.py`)

The delay/oblateness caveats were sized against a 0.14 headline; against +0.02–0.06 they
needed re-measurement. Both audits are paired per seed (seed noise cancels in the shift);
`mcrt.rotating.travel_time_delay` (eq. 18 quadrature, generalized out of the C1 driver;
flat-space closed form Δt = (R/c)(1 − cos α) pinned in the tests) supplies the warp.

**C4.1 Light-travel-time delay — ESCALATED, now included.** The warp Δφ = ν·Δt(α) is
exactly PF-invariant for a single spot (measured: <2e-6, the interpolation floor) but does
**not** commute with the two-spot sum. Against the diluted numbers it is 2–5σ — and
*positive*: de-synchronizing the spots' extremes partially restores the PF gap:

| ΔPF(346.5 Hz) | δ⁴ no warp | δ⁴ + warp | band-ph no warp | **band-ph + warp (all-geometry)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Riley | +0.0371 | +0.0414 (2.1σ shift) | +0.0192 | **+0.0302** (5.4σ shift) |
| Miller | +0.0612 | +0.0692 (2.5σ shift) | +0.0446 | **+0.0560** (4.3σ shift) |

Per the C4 rule (>σ ⇒ model it, don't caveat it), the quoted rotating residuals now
include the warp: **+0.030 (Riley) / +0.056 (Miller)** band-photon. The shape-routing
metrics are unchanged by the warp (shape RMS 0.10, Δ(A1/A0) +0.024/+0.021) — it enters
both models identically at the waveform level, so only the min/max statistic feels it.
Note the irony: the warp *reduces* how much of the dilution survives to the final number —
the two geometry effects (boost saturation, warp de-synchronization) partially offset.

**C4.2 Oblateness — caveat STANDS, now with a measured bound.** AM14 first-order
perturbation (Ω̄² ≈ 0.033–0.050, o₂ ≈ −1.7%/−2.5% pole flattening; near-equatorial spots ⇒
spot-local ΔR/R ≤ 0.2%, normal tilts ≤ 0.6°): paired ΔPF shift **+0.0011 (0.5σ) Riley,
−0.00002 (0.01σ) Miller**. The unmodelled remainder (oblate bending/Jacobian, OS-level)
is of the same order. One caveat sentence with these numbers attached suffices.

## 6. The physics — why the boost *dilutes* the beaming systematic

Both limb-darkening **and** Doppler boost are *pulse sharpeners*, acting on different phases:

- **Limb-darkening** is *symmetric*: it dims *both* limbs (low μ) and peaks at face-on. It
  sharpens by brightening the facing phase relative to the grazing limbs — this *is* the ΔPF
  mechanism.
- **Doppler boost** is *asymmetric* and **phase-offset**: it peaks a quarter-turn *before*
  face-on (the approaching limb), is ~neutral at face-on (transverse, δ = 1/γ), and troughs a
  quarter-turn *after* (receding limb).

The boost is strongest **at the limb phases** — which is exactly where limb-darkening has
already dimmed the *realistic* pulse. So (δ⁴, Riley; an earlier draft of this section quoted
the δ³ values 0.34/0.37 here — corrected):
- In the **realistic** pulse, Doppler is boosting an already-dark phase → limited absolute
  effect. PF rises only 0.31 → 0.42.
- In the **isotropic** pulse, those limb phases are still moderately bright, so the boost adds
  a big new swing. PF rises 0.17 → 0.39.

The two pulses **converge toward a common Doppler-dominated shape, and the gap between them —
ΔPF — collapses.** Equivalently: at high spin, Doppler does most of the sharpening
limb-darkening used to do, so the *marginal* value of the beaming assumption shrinks. Two
partially-redundant sharpeners; in the real pulse, limb-darkening got to the limb phases first.

Per the plan's rule (`|coupling| ≤ σ` → caveat; `> σ` → quantified correction), this is
**37σ → a quantified correction, not a caveat.** But a "correction" that cuts the headline

## 8. Decisions — RESOLVED (2026-07-07)

1. **δ⁴ energy vs δ³ photon — superseded.** The C3 band machinery contains both as wide-band
   limits; the NICER-faithful number is the in-band photon count (§5c). The δⁿ rows remain as

   The Track-F bolometric-scope guard paragraph now gains a sentence: the band treatment is a

   violation.
3. **Framing — the ROUTING REFRAME, upgraded by §5b.** Keep +0.137/+0.195 as the bolometric
   beaming *mechanism*; report **two routers** of where it appears: geometry (tiling → PF;
   eclipse/dark gap → shape) and spin (boost saturates the min/max statistic → shape). The
   conserved quantity is the waveform-level difference (~10 % RMS, spin- and band-invariant);
   the PF readout retains +0.02–0.06 at the real spin depending on convention/band. This is
   a stronger paper than either the original headline or the "replace" option: PF is shown to
   be a *spin-degraded probe* of beaming, and the four-pulse figure makes it visual.
