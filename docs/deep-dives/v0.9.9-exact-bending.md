## 1. Why the linear map was the biggest open risk

A photon leaves the surface at emission angle **α** (from the local radial normal) and reaches
the observer at geometric angle **ψ** (between spot normal and line of sight). Gravity bends the
path, so ψ > α, and the whole pulse model hinges on the map **cos α(cos ψ)**.

Every result to date used the **Beloborodov (2002)** linear approximation

    cos α = u + (1 − u) cos ψ,      Jacobian  d cos α / d cos ψ = (1 − u) = const,

quoted "exact to ~1% for u ≲ 0.5." Two facts made it the leading systematic risk (attention
item 1):

1. Its error is **largest at grazing emission** (α → 90°, cos α → 0) — and that is *exactly*
   where J0740's faint phase lives (μ_min ≈ 0.005), which sets F_min, hence PF.
2. J0740 (Riley) sits at **u = 0.494**, the edge of the approximation's stated validity, while
   the only prior bending validation (SD1a) was at the gentler u ≈ 0.34.

So the linear map was doing the most load-bearing work precisely where it is least trustworthy.
B1 replaces it with the truth; B2 measures what that costs.

## 2. The exact map — `src/mcrt/bending.py`

The exact Schwarzschild deflection is the standard quadrature (Pechenick, Ftaclas & Cohen 1983),
recast with `x = R/r` and `β = b/R = sin α / √(1 − u)` to remove the improper upper limit:

    ψ(α) = ∫_0^1  β dx / √(1 − β² x² (1 − u x)).

Implementation notes:

- **`deflection_angle(α, u)`** integrates this by **Gauss–Legendre quadrature** — numpy only,
  no scipy (matching `theory.chandrasekhar_h`'s hand-rolled `leggauss` precedent). GL nodes are
  strictly interior, so the integrable √-singularity at the x = 1 grazing endpoint is never
  evaluated. At u = 0 the integrand collapses to `arcsin`, giving ψ(α) = α exactly (the
  flat-space check).
- **`ExactBending(u)`** tabulates ψ(α) on a dense α grid, then inverts the monotone map to
  `cos_alpha(cos ψ)` (via `np.interp` on the reversed, ascending table) and exposes the exact
  **lensing Jacobian** `D(ψ) = d cos α / d cos ψ` (via `np.gradient`). D replaces the linear
  map's constant `(1 − u)` in the flux weight `F ∝ D · I(cos α) · cos α`.
- **Eclipse handling:** below the visibility threshold the map linearly extrapolates cos α < 0,
  so the existing `cos α ≥ 0` visibility clip in `pulse.point_spot_flux` fires unchanged.
- **Guard:** the constructor rejects `u ≥ 2/3` (surface at/inside the photon sphere,
  r = 3GM/c²), where the integral diverges — a physics limit, not a resolution one, flagged with
  a clear message rather than a silent NaN.

How different is the exact map? The exact–linear gap in cos α over the visible range grows sharply
with compactness — small at SD1a, large at J0740:

| u | ψ_max (grazing) | max \|cos α_exact − cos α_linear\| |
|---|---|---|
| 0.3445 (SD1a) | 120.5° | 0.011 |
| 0.444 (J0740 Miller) | 138.0° | 0.031 |
| 0.494 (J0740 Riley) | 150.6° | 0.053 |

## 3. Validation — 24 tests, all green (`tests/test_bending.py`)

The load-bearing checks are **external references**, not internal self-consistency:

| Check | Result |
|---|---|
| Flat-space limit ψ(α) = α at u → 0 | matches to ~1e-9 on the non-grazing interior |
| Independent high-res trapezoid of the same integral | agrees to < 1e-4 |
| Jacobian vs an **independently-derived analytic** dcos α/dcos ψ | ~1e-4, incl. grazing (cos α = 0.005): 0.7348 vs 0.7346 |
| `D(cos ψ = 1)` vs the linear constant `(1 − u)` | **exact** (self-consistency anchor) |
| Beloborodov agreement at SD1a u = 0.34 | < 1.2% in cos α; gap **grows** with u (locks item 1's premise) |
| **SD1a waveform vs the independent IM code** | **max\|Δ\| 0.80% → 0.11%** (see below) |
| Quadrature convergence, monotonicity, guard, mismatch-rejection | pass |

**The free self-check (the headline validation).** Re-running the v0.8.1 SD1a code-comparison
(our point-spot waveform vs the independent Illinois–Maryland ray-tracer, u ≈ 0.3445) with exact
bending **shrinks the residual from 0.80% to 0.11%**. This confirms, against an outside code, that
the ~1% SD1a gap *was* the Beloborodov approximation — and the exact map nearly closes it. It is
the strongest evidence the new map is correct, and it came for free from an existing benchmark.

## 4. Integration — opt-in, linear default untouched

`point_spot_flux` and `compute_profile` gained an optional **`bending=`** argument:

- `bending=None` (default) → the verified linear `bend` path, **bit-for-bit identical** to
  pre-v0.9.9 (asserted by test: same flux, same `(1 − u)` Jacobian).
- `bending=ExactBending(u)` → exact map *and* exact Jacobian. A `_bend_and_jacobian` helper
  routes between them and **rejects a compactness mismatch** (an exact map built for a different
  u than the flux is computed at would silently corrupt the geometry).

Exports: `ExactBending`, `bend_exact`, `deflection_angle` added to `mcrt`.

## 5. The measured shift + Gate G1 — **B2 done**

B2 re-ran both J0740 anchors through the *same* per-seed pipeline that produced the linear
headline (A3), once with the linear map and once with exact bending, on the production 5-seed
library (`scripts/b2_exact_bending.py`, reproduces the linear branch bit-for-bit as its control).
The measured shift on the **real `I(μ; τ)`**, at the headline τ = 10:

| anchor | u | ΔPF_linear ± σ | ΔPF_exact ± σ | shift | \|shift\|/σ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Riley** (X-PSI ST-U) | 0.494 | +0.1437 ± 0.0032 | **+0.1371 ± 0.0027** | **−0.0066** | **2.1σ** |
| **Miller** (Illinois–Maryland) | 0.444 | +0.2012 ± 0.0052 | **+0.1952 ± 0.0053** | −0.0060 | 1.1σ |

Both shifts are **negative** — the linear map slightly *over*-states the pulsed fraction at
grazing emission, and exact bending pulls it back. The shift is **resolution-stable to 5 decimals**
(−0.00660 across n_alpha = 2048…16384) and vanishes in the flat limit (shift = 0.000 at u = 0.05,
same geometry), so it is the physics, not a tabulation artifact.

**Why this shift matters — statistical vs. systematic error.** The **± σ is the seed error bar**:
the library `I(μ; τ)` is a histogram of a *finite* number (4×10⁵) of randomly-scattered escaped
photons, so 5 independent RNG seeds give 5 slightly different curves and 5 slightly different ΔPF;
their spread is σ ≈ 0.003 (Riley). That is **statistical noise** — no preferred direction, shrinks
as ∝ 1/√N (more photons).

The linear→exact shift is a **systematic** of an entirely different kind: a fixed, directional
bias from a *modeling approximation*, and **no amount of extra computation removes it** — running
more photons tightens the ± σ but leaves the −0.0066 untouched. At Riley the shift is **2.1σ** —
*larger than the error bar we quote*. That is what **Gate G1** keys on (magnitude vs σ, not sign):

- shift ≤ σ → the linear map's error hides under the noise → keep Beloborodov, quote one sentence.
- shift > σ → the linear map biases the headline beyond its own error bar → **report the exact
  number** for the anchors + phase-diagram star markers; the broad phase-diagram sweep stays linear
  with a stated tolerance.

**Gate G1 verdict: SWITCH to exact.** Riley's 2.1σ clears the bar, so the honest headline for the
J0740 anchors is the **bending-corrected** number:

> **ΔPF(J0740, τ = 10) = +0.137 ± 0.003 (Riley), +0.195 ± 0.005 (Miller)** — exact Schwarzschild
> bending. (Linear: +0.144 / +0.201; the ~1% approximation cost the headline ≈ 0.006–0.007 in PF,
> ~2σ at the most compact anchor.)

The systematic remains the same size, sign, and geometry-routing as before — the correction is a
tightening, not a reversal. Note the production shift (−0.0066) is *smaller* than the B1 Eddington
preview (−0.010): the production `I(μ; τ)` is less limb-brightened at grazing than the Eddington
`1 + 1.5μ` stand-in, so it leans on the least-trustworthy part of the linear map less hard — but
the shift still clears σ, so the verdict is unchanged from the preview.

**A second, qualitative correction — the visibility horizon (and why it *strengthens* the thesis).**
The linear map does not just misprice the flux; it misplaces the horizon. Beloborodov's `cos α`
extrapolates *past grazing*, claiming a spot stays visible out to ψ = 167.5° (u = 0.494), whereas
the exact grazing horizon — the largest ψ any real emission angle α ≤ 90° reaches — is only
ψ = 150.6°. In that 17° gap the linear map returns a spurious small-positive `cos α`. The
consequence at Riley: the v0.9.2 statement *"neither spot ever sets (μ_min ≈ 0.005)"* was a linear
artifact — under exact bending **each spot eclipses ≈ 14%** of the cycle (Miller: ≈ 23%, essentially
unchanged since it already dipped well past grazing). **But the headline conclusion holds and
sharpens:** the two spots are anti-phased, so the *combined* flux floor barely moves
(0.698 → 0.706 for Riley) — when one spot sets the other faces us. PF stays unsaturated, ΔPF stays
live (+0.137), and Riley now looks like Miller (both eclipse, both tile). The paper's discriminator —
**tiling, not single-spot eclipse, decides where the systematic lands** — is reinforced: even the
"never-sets" star turns out to eclipse, and tiling still carries it into the pulsed fraction.

**Follow-through (all confirmed):**

- **J0030 (eclipsing) is unmoved.** At u = 0.312 (Riley) and 0.326 (Miller) the flux floor stays
  0 and ΔPF stays exactly 0 under *both* maps — PF is pinned by the dark gap regardless of bending,
  so the shape-only verdict there is bending-independent and no anchor decision hangs on it.
- **Phase-diagram star markers.** A marker's (Δφ, θ_mean) coordinates are bending-independent; only
  its ΔPF *value* shifts — by ≤ 0.007 for the two J0740 markers (invisible on the heatmap) and
  exactly 0 for the two J0030 markers. Every star stays firmly on its side of the tiling/saturation
  boundary, so the qualitative claim is untouched. The markers are now drawn with exact bending
  (below); the broad heatmap behind them stays linear (stated tolerance: exact–linear ΔPF gap
  ≤ 0.007 at the u = 0.49 panel, smaller elsewhere).

**Gate enacted (v0.9.9.1).** The switch is now live in the pipeline, not just recorded here:

- `scripts/j0740_anchor.py` — `run()` builds an `ExactBending(u)` per anchor; the standalone
  figure and `data/j0740_anchor.npz` are exact. (`sweep_anchor` keeps an opt-in `bending=` so the
  linear control is still reachable.)
- `scripts/phase_diagram.py` — `star_marker` uses exact bending per star; the broad sweep stays
  linear.
- `scripts/a3_seed_errors.py` — **deliberately stays linear**: its gate checks Riley against the
  v0.9.7 convergence study (+0.1486), itself linear, so the check is only meaningful
  linear-vs-linear. It remains the linear self-consistency cross-check and the J0030 shape-RMS ± σ
  source; the exact per-seed headline lives in `b2_exact_bending.py`.

**The +0.137 is the 0 Hz (non-rotating) baseline.** Gate G1 fixes the *bending* map; the
*rotational* corrections (Doppler + aberration, Track C / Gate G2) are a separate, additive layer
measured *on top of* this exact-bending baseline as the difference-of-differences
ΔPF(346.5 Hz) − ΔPF(0 Hz). So the fully final headline is `+0.137 + (G2 coupling)`; C2 re-confirms
the exact number once it exists. G1 and G2 are orthogonal gates on different approximations — G1 is
closed here, G2 stacks on it, and B2 is seconds-cheap to re-run if the library ever changes under D1.

**Reproduce:** `PYTHONPATH=src:scripts python3 scripts/b2_exact_bending.py` → `data/b2_exact_bending.npz`
(headline ± σ); `python3 scripts/j0740_anchor.py` / `phase_diagram.py` regenerate the exact figures.
