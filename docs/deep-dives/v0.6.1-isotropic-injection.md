## 1. The fix

v0.6.0 traced the unphysical thin-Žä limb brightening to the boundary source: the engine injected
photons **uniformly in ╬╝**, which is isotropic per *solid angle* but not isotropic in *specific
intensity*. The thermal source at the base of the atmosphere is isotropic in intensity, so the
fix is a single line in `src/mcrt/monte_carlo.py`:

```python
# before ŌĆö isotropic in solid angle (over-produces grazing photons)
costheta = np.random.uniform(0, 1)

# after ŌĆö isotropic in specific intensity, N(mu) ŌłØ mu
costheta = np.sqrt(np.random.uniform(0, 1))
```

---

## 2. Why `sqrt(U)`

A surface that is equally bright in every direction (isotropic specific intensity, `I = const`)
does not emit equal *numbers* of photons in every direction. The count crossing the boundary into
a direction is the brightness times the `cos ╬Ė = ╬╝` projection (foreshortening):

$$ N(\mu)\,d\mu \;\propto\; I(\mu)\,\mu\,d\mu \;=\; \text{const}\cdot\mu\,d\mu. $$

So an isotropic-intensity source emits with the probability density `f(╬╝) ŌłØ ╬╝` on `[0, 1]`. To
draw such samples from a uniform `U Ōłł [0, 1]`, invert the cumulative distribution: the fraction
of photons below some ╬╝ is `Ōł½ŌéĆ^╬╝ 2╬╝' d╬╝' = ╬╝┬▓`, so setting `╬╝┬▓ = U` gives `╬╝ = sqrt(U)`. Uniform
sampling (`f(╬╝) = const`) would instead correspond to `I(╬╝) ŌłØ 1/╬╝` ŌĆö the limb-brightened source
v0.6.0 was unintentionally modelling.

This is the same projection factor as the v0.5.1 fluxŌåÆintensity correction, applied at the *other*
end of the photon's life: ╬╝-weighting governs both how an isotropic source *emits* and how escape
counts must be *un*-weighted to recover intensity.

## 3. The corrected library

Rebuilding the library (`scripts/tau_sweep.py`) with the fixed injection:

![Beaming-function library: I(╬╝) across optical depths Žä](../../data/beaming_tau_curves.png)

![Limb-darkening slope b vs. optical depth Žä](../../data/beaming_slope_vs_tau.png)

| Žä | 0.1 | 0.3 | 1.0 | 3.0 | 10.0 | 30.0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `b` (v0.6.0, uniform) | ŌłÆ0.88 | ŌłÆ0.53 | +0.53 | +1.66 | +1.69 | +1.75 |
| `b` (v0.6.1, sqrt(U)) | **+0.29** | **+0.73** | **+1.44** | **+1.69** | **+1.79** | **+1.67** |

The slope now **rises monotonically** from near-isotropic at thin Žä, crosses the Eddington value
(`b = 1.5`) around Žä Ōēł 1, and plateaus near the Chandrasekhar regime (`b Ōēł 1.7`ŌĆō`1.8`) for Žä Ōēź 3.
Every value is positive ŌĆö the limb brightening is gone, and the family of `I(╬╝)` curves all darken
toward the limb as physics requires.

**Two honest caveats:**

- **Thin Žä is small-but-nonzero, not exactly 0.** At Žä = 0.1, `b Ōēł 0.29` rather than 0, because
  even a thin atmosphere scatters a little and imparts mild limb darkening. The `b ŌåÆ 0` (perfectly
  isotropic) limit is the Žä ŌåÆ 0 idealisation; Žä = 0.1 sitting just above it is correct.
- **The Žä = 30 point dips slightly** (1.79 ŌåÆ 1.67). Only ~8,500 of 200k photons escape at Žä = 30,
  so the low-╬╝ tail bins are noise-limited. This is exactly the regime the upcoming convergence
  study must size N for; it is statistics, not a physics regression.
